02. Project Reviews

Project Reviews

Some of the most important feedback that you will receive from mentors at Udacity will be in the form of the reviews on your project submissions.

Projects are key milestones in your Nanodegree program and have detailed requirements and instructions. Once you have completed all of the components of the project you simply submit your project from the classroom for a review.

Unless otherwise noted in the classroom, we try to return your project review to you via email within 24 hours of your submission.

The reviewer will comment on which rubric items you passed or failed and will provide personalized suggestions for improvement or resources for further learning.

You may continue to re-submit the project until you pass all of the requirements. You can view an example code review below.

Many students tell us that they find project reviews from our certified project reviewers to be the most helpful component of their learning.

Udacity reviewers work hard to help you improve and have a 4.9/5 average rating for over 2k projects each day.

More than 95% of students who submit a project eventually pass. The secret is to keep trying and never give up!

Before you submit your project you should ensure that it meets all of the specifications detailed on the project rubric available in the classroom. If you have any questions regarding the lessons leading up to the project, or the project itself, then you should use Student Hub to reach out to your mentor and other classmates or check Knowledge to see if the issue has already been addressed.
