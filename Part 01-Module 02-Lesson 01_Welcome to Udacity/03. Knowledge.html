03. Knowledge

Knowledge

Knowledge is a platform where you can search for already answered questions, answer questions from other students, or even ask your own questions! It is supported by Udacity mentors, other students, and alumni.

When you have a question, we suggest that you search for an answer via Knowledge first. It may have already been answered, which makes Knowledge one of the best places to find answers fast!

If you don't see your question, simply post it to Knowledge. You are likely to get an answer within 24 hours and you'll be helping future students who are likely to encounter the same problem.

We see our most successful students learning from helping others.

If you see a question posted that you know the answer to then we encourage you to post a solution!

It's a great chance to participate in the discussion while learning more and helping future students.

Knowledge displays search results and answers, and moderates posts based on how useful they are.

Our goal is for all content on Knowledge to be structured, searchable, and useful to other students.

When you use Knowledge, please remember to upvote or downvote questions and answers to help build the community make it as useful as possible for others.

Please note that if you're having trouble with your Udacity account, you should not go to Student Hub or Knowledge. Please see the "Get Help with Your Account" lesson for more information!
